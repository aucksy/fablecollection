Armature

Five mechanical watch faces — skeletons, gauges and instruments — built on the one motion Watch Face Format actually has: rotation, driven natively by the clock itself. A "hand" can carry any artwork — a gear, an orbiting orb, a needle — so gears turn, orbs orbit and needles sweep with zero expressions and zero risk. Everything that mechanical watches do that WFF can't (oscillating balance wheels, rolling counters, counter-rotating trains) is designed out, honestly, before a single line is built.

Rotation is native

AnalogClock hands are platform-driven and smooth — and a hand can be a gear, a disc or an orb. The whole series is built on that one proven engine.

No motion cosplay

No oscillating escapements, no rolling digit wheels, no fly-back sweeps. If a mechanism can't rotate at a clock rate, it isn't in the design.

Ship-safe by construction

Every signature maps to an on-wrist-proven pattern: native hands + Sweep, Transform arc fills, minute-stepped angle transforms, alpha gates.

OLED economics

Skeletons and gauges are strokes on black — cheap to draw, dramatic in the metal, and easy to collapse into a ≤15%-lit ambient variant.

How to read the feasibility badges
NATIVE

Built into Watch Face Format itself and drawn by a mechanism named in the row — AnalogClock hands, Sweep, arc-fill Transforms, TimeText, alpha gates. Ships in the face with no dependencies.

PROVIDER

Data arrives through standard Wear OS complication providers. Defaults are restricted to the WFF v1 system-provider list; heart-rate and weather can be user-added but can never be a default.

SIMULATED

Shown live in this prototype with generated values so you can judge the design. The real face binds the same slot to a real provider. Nothing renders here that the real WFF face couldn't draw.

CUSTOM

Would need our own complication provider or companion app. In ARMATURE this badge appears only on optional, non-signature extras — no face depends on one to ship.

⚠ build cost — rows carrying this note are possible-but-costly: the mechanism is real, but it multiplies elements/assets or needs one dedicated on-wrist verification round. Costed here so nothing is discovered after building.

Designed inside the format — the mechanical walls ARMATURE was built against
Oscillating balance wheel / tick-tock escapement

WFF has no reciprocating or sinusoidal motion — only uniform rotation at clock rates. The "escapement" is a uniformly turning gear (one revolution per minute, platform-driven), and the specs say so.

Rolling odometer digit wheels

Digits change by instant substitution — no scroll, no roll, no tween. ODOMETER leans into the jump: framed counter windows where the platform's instant substitution IS the mechanism's aesthetic.

Counter-rotating meshed gear pairs

Smooth rotation exists only clockwise at hour/minute/second rates (native hands). A counter-rotating partner could only step once per second — visibly mismatched. Gear trains run same-direction at different clock rates.

Animated retrograde fly-back

A retrograde needle's return at :00 is an instant jump (the value changes; nothing animates). SECTOR's scale is designed so the jump home reads as intentional, and the spec states it.

Redline zones that "light up"

No conditionals in colour attributes; a needle can't recolour by value. Redline zones are printed statically on the scale; state-colouring anything means duplicated copies gated on a Boolean — budgeted, or cut.

Moving complication slots

Slot geometry is static — a slot can't ride a rotating bezel or slide. Gauges fill via proven arc Transforms inside fixed slots; nothing translates.

Engraved curved / letterspaced lettering

No curved text and no letterSpacing exist. All labels are straight, tight-set type; a short label may be rotated as one block.

Bright metal plates in always-on

Ambient must stay ≤15% lit pixels. Every AOD here collapses plates and skeletons to hairlines in fixed light ink — the Series-01-proven thin-twin pattern.

About this prototype
This page is a design simulator, not a build. The clock is your real local time and all complication values are generated placeholders. No Wear OS project, APK or production code exists for ARMATURE yet — that is a deliberate Phase-1 gate. Like the LEDGER showcase, this simulator is feasibility-locked: every rotating element moves exactly the way a native AnalogClock hand would, the retrograde needle steps once per minute and jumps home instantly, digits substitute without transitions, and every rendered element corresponds to a named WFF v1 mechanism already proven on a real watch in Series 01. If it renders here, it can ship.
